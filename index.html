Nutrition Tips

Eat a Rainbow

Consume a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables to ensure you get a wide range of nutrients.

  • Red: Tomatoes, strawberries (lycopene, anthocyanins)
  • Orange/Yellow: Carrots, oranges (beta-carotene, vitamin C)
  • Green: Spinach, broccoli (lutein, folate)
  • Blue/Purple: Blueberries, eggplant (anthocyanins)

Stay Hydrated

Water is essential for nearly every bodily function. Aim for 8-10 glasses daily.

Signs of dehydration:

  • Fatigue
  • Headaches
  • Dry skin
  • Dark urine

Limit Processed Foods

Processed foods often contain excessive salt, sugar, and unhealthy fats.

Tips to reduce processed foods:

  • Cook meals at home
  • Read nutrition labels
  • Choose whole foods
  • Prepare healthy snacks in advance

Exercise Recommendations

Cardiovascular Exercise

Benefits for heart health and endurance:

  • Brisk walking (30 min/day)
  • Swimming (20-30 min, 3x/week)
  • Cycling (30 min, moderate pace)
  • Dancing (fun way to get moving)

Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity per week.

Strength Training

Builds muscle and increases metabolism:

  • Bodyweight exercises (push-ups, squats)
  • Resistance bands
  • Free weights (start light)
  • Yoga/Pilates (improves flexibility too)

2-3 sessions per week targeting all major muscle groups.

Flexibility & Balance

Important for injury prevention and mobility:

  • Daily stretching routine
  • Tai Chi (excellent for balance)
  • Yoga (combines strength and flexibility)
  • Balance exercises (single-leg stands)

Especially important as we age.

Mental Wellbeing

Stress Management

Effective techniques to reduce stress:

  • Deep breathing exercises
  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Mindfulness meditation (10 min/day)
  • Regular physical activity
  • Adequate sleep (7-9 hours)

Positive Habits

Build resilience and emotional wellbeing:

  • Gratitude journaling
  • Social connections
  • Time in nature
  • Creative outlets
  • Setting healthy boundaries

When to Seek Help

Signs you might benefit from professional support:

  • Persistent sadness or anxiety
  • Difficulty with daily functioning
  • Changes in sleep/appetite
  • Withdrawal from activities
  • Thoughts of self-harm

Mental health is just as important as physical health.
